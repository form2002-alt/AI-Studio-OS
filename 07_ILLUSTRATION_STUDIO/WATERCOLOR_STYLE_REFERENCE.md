# Watercolor Style Reference

Updated: 2026-07-06  
Related file: `07_ILLUSTRATION_STUDIO/STYLE_SYSTEM_REFERENCE.md`

---

## Priority Rule

When the user simply asks for `수채화`, `부드러운 손그림`, `전시용 수채 인물`, or `현대적인 수채화`, apply this style first unless the user specifies another style.

**Standard style name:** `현대적 담백 수채화 + 얇은 라인아트`

---

## Use Case

Exhibition panels, public relations halls, museum character graphics, educational figures, cultural storytelling illustrations, regional history graphics, citizen-life illustrations, and soft background images for exhibition storytelling.

---

## Visual Direction

A modern, calm, refined watercolor style with thin line art, broad transparent washes, restrained details, pale low-saturation colors, and museum-appropriate clarity.

This direction must avoid old-fashioned, overly busy watercolor effects. It should not rely on many tiny brush strokes. Use large painted areas, soft tonal washes, controlled value gradation, clear readable forms, and visible white space.

---

## Key Features

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| Line | Thin, restrained ink or pencil-like outline. Use clear contour only where readability requires it. Avoid heavy black contours. |
| Color | Pale, muted, low-saturation colors. Prefer beige, pale ochre, muted brown, soft blue-gray, light green, and gentle skin tones. Avoid dark or heavily saturated colors. |
| Wash | Broad transparent watercolor washes. Use large shapes and soft gradation. Avoid excessive tiny brush marks. |
| Detail | Moderate detail only. Keep the figure readable from a distance. Do not over-render clothing folds, skin, hair, or accessories. |
| Face and hands | Minimal line and color information. Avoid photorealistic facial detail, pores, heavy wrinkles, and excessive eyelashes. |
| Clothing | Use large value shapes and soft gradation. Keep folds simple and educationally readable. |
| Paper texture | Allow subtle paper grain, white gaps, and natural watercolor edges. Do not make the texture messy. |
| Background | Mostly blank white or very light wash. Do not use complex scenery unless required by the topic. |
| Mood | Quiet, humane, refined, dignified, educational, museum-appropriate. |

## Negative Direction

Avoid heavy black outlines, dark saturated colors, excessive tiny brushwork, old-fashioned busy brush strokes, photorealistic skin, pores, excessive wrinkles, over-detailed hands, messy background, random text, logos, watermarks, decorative clutter, glossy 3D rendering, and dramatic hyperrealism.

굵고 진한 검은 외곽선 금지, 어둡고 과포화된 색 금지, 자잘한 붓터치 과다 금지, 오래된 느낌의 과도한 수채 세부 묘사 금지, 실사풍 피부 묘사 금지, 모공·주름 과다 금지, 손 디테일 과다 금지, 복잡하고 지저분한 배경 금지, 무작위 글자·로고·워터마크 금지, 장식 과다 금지, 3D 렌더링 느낌 금지

## Production Note

This style is the preferred watercolor direction for exhibition and public-relations figure illustrations. It is suitable when the goal is clarity, dignity, warmth, and visual softness rather than dramatic realism. For historically specific Korean subjects, combine this style with separate accuracy rules for period, clothing, footwear, tools, occupation, and cultural context.
